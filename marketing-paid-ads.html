Paid ads, explained for this business

What paid advertising is, how it works for a slow, high-value sale like ours, the exact channels and budgets, and ready-to-run ads. Read this before spending a dollar.

Sora Casas·Internal·June 2026·Full data: sora-real-estate/03-paid-ads.md

1. The one thing to understand first

We are not selling a $30 product where someone clicks an ad and buys today. We sell a home that costs a few hundred thousand dollars, to a foreigner, in another country. The journey from "saw the ad" to "wired the money" runs 6 to 18 months. That single fact changes everything about how we advertise.

It means paid ads do three different jobs, and you need all three:

  1. Capture the few people searching right now (Google Search). High intent, higher cost, lower volume.
  2. Seed the much larger group who are dreaming, not yet searching (Meta and YouTube). Cheap attention, builds a warm audience you re-show ads to for months.
  3. Prove you're real and trustworthy (newsletters, podcasts, testimonials, in-person). This is what closes a high-trust sale.

A common mistake is spending everything on job 1 because it feels measurable, getting a few expensive leads, and concluding ads don't work. They do, but only when all three jobs run together over months.

2. The only numbers that matter

Forget clicks and impressions as goals. The chain that matters runs: ad spend, then a lead, then a qualified lead, then a discovery call, then a site visit, then a contract. The further down you measure, the truer the number. Our north star is cost per discovery call, then cost per booked site visit. Realistic targets for us:

MetricTargetWhy
Cost per lead (form fill)$75 to $200Vanity-ish; easy to get cheap junk leads
Cost per qualified lead$150 to $900Has budget, timeline, fit
Cost per discovery call$250 to $500The real efficiency metric
Cost per booked site visit~$2,500The bottleneck (needs a flight)
Cost per buyer~$7,500On a $400K sale = ~1% of revenue. Excellent.

The biggest leak in this kind of business is the jump from discovery call to site visit, because it requires the buyer to book a flight and take a week off. The single best money you can spend is a $1,500 flight credit applied to their home at signing. Roughly a quarter to 40% of people who fly down end up buying, so every extra visit is worth about $80K in expected revenue. That credit pays for itself many times over.

Three budget tiers, ramping as you prove it works. Full tables in 03-paid-ads.md; the shape:

Start at $5K. Do not jump to $30K before a smaller budget proves the funnel converts. Spending more on a broken funnel just loses money faster.

4. Google Search: capture in-market buyers

Bid on terms where someone is ready to act, not researching. Expect $4 to $9 per click for these (expat English searches behave like finance/legal bids).

Bid on: custom home builder Panama · build a house in Panama · Boquete / Pedasí / Volcán real estate · Friendly Nations Visa real estate · Panama Qualified Investor Visa · buy land Panama.

Do NOT bid on (write content instead): retire in Panama · cost of living Panama · is Panama safe. These are research intent, expensive, and convert poorly. They're SEO targets, not ad targets.

Negative keywords to add day one so you don't pay for the wrong "Panama": -"city beach" -florida -jobs -rental -hotel -papers please -canal cruise.

7. Glossary (so the dashboards make sense)

CPC cost per click. What you pay each time someone clicks the ad.
CPM cost per thousand impressions. Used for awareness/video, not leads.
CPL cost per lead. Spend divided by form fills. Easy to game with low-quality leads.
CPA / CAC cost per acquisition / customer acquisition cost. What it costs to get one buyer.
CR conversion rate. Share of visitors who take the action (lead, call). Ours: aim 5 to 8% on landing pages.
Lookalike (LAL) an audience the platform builds to resemble your best existing leads/buyers. The most powerful Meta tool once you have a lead list.
Retargeting showing ads to people who already visited the site or watched a video. Cheap, high-converting, runs continuously.
Performance Max (PMax) Google's automated campaign across all its surfaces. Use for retargeting first, not cold.
UTM tags little labels on a link (utm_source, utm_campaign) so analytics shows which ad sent which lead. Always use them.

8. When you're ready, the first 30 days

  1. Make sure tracking is live: GA4 (done), Meta Pixel + Google Enhanced Conversions (add the IDs to leads.js).
  2. Pick 2 landing pages (the build-cost guide + the FNV post are ready).
  3. $5K month: Google Search (3 ad groups above) + Meta (2 ads above, cold) + a little YouTube retargeting.
  4. Watch cost per discovery call, not clicks. Kill what's above $500, double what's below.
  5. Turn on the $1,500 flight credit in retargeting once you have site-visit candidates.
