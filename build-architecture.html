Architecture, engineering & permits

Updated 2026-06-24 · Owner: Abraham · INTERNAL build playbook (3 of 4)
↖ Overview→ Cost & budget→ Process & timeline→ Materials & finishes→ Contractor & team
The rule that governs everything: a foreign buyer can absolutely build in Panama, but every project must run through a Panama-licensed professional (an idóneo). A foreign architect can design it, but a local idóneo architect must sign and seal the plans (and bears sole legal responsibility), with separate idóneos for structural, electrical, and sanitary sheets. The licensing body is JTIA (the state regulator); SPIA is the professional guild (it publishes recommended fees, it does not license).

The idóneos (who signs what)

ProfessionalCodeProduces
Arquitecto idóneoAAnteproyecto, planos arquitectónicos (plantas, fachadas, cortes, techos, acabados), specs. The legal "technical guardian."
Ingeniero estructuralESPlanos estructurales + memoria de cálculo estructural (signs every page); requires the soil study.
Ingeniero eléctricoELPlanos eléctricos per the RIE; approved by the electric distributor + Bomberos (valid ~2 yr).
Ing. sanitario / plomeroPLPlanos sanitarios/hidráulicos + memorias; reviewed by MINSA + IDAAN (valid ~3 yr).

Foreign design → built via refrendo: a foreign architect's design can be built, but the local idóneo who signs and seals is the only responsible party. Expect a Panamanian architect to charge a ~$1,500 minimum just to translate, adapt to seismic/block code, and stamp (plus separate EL/PL stamps). Sora takeaway: don't pursue foreign idoneidad (very restrictive — only via marriage/child/naturalization + degree reválida); retain local idóneos to refrendar and adapt.

Deliverables & the 3D model

  • Anteproyecto (schematic): plans, elevations, a memoria — the feasibility + client-communication stage where the 3D/glb model lives. Registered via the municipal RLA before construction docs. Not buildable from.
  • Planos / proyecto de ejecución (buildable set): exact dimensions, full structural + MEP calcs, quantities/budget. Architectural + structural + memoria de cálculo + hydraulic + sanitary + electrical + budget.
  • Software: AutoCAD (2D/3D standard), Revit/ArchiCAD (BIM), SketchUp (massing). glb pipeline: source model → export glTF/glb (SketchUp/Blender native; Revit needs an exporter) → WebGL viewer or <model-viewer> embed. The glb belongs at the anteproyecto gate for sign-off — it is a sales/visualization tool, not a regulatory deliverable.
  • Code: the structural code is REP-2021 (REP-2014 superseded), referencing ACI 318 / AISC 360 / ASCE 7; the memoria must demonstrate seismic + wind compliance — important near Barú (Boquete/Volcán) and on liquefiable coastal sand.

Soil study (estudio de suelos)

Not a standalone permit but a required input, practically needed for any ground-floor-plus-one-story or sloped/unstable lot (most custom homes). ~US$2,590, ~2–8 weeks (anchor figure, confirm locally). Done by a geotechnical idóneo + lab (Tecnilab, Geolabs, Ingeniería Prego, UTP LABGEO). It produces bearing capacity + the foundation recommendation, and can downgrade an over-cautious pile design to ordinary zapatas, saving real money. Coastal risk: shallow water table, liquefaction, saline corrosion. Highland risk: collapse-prone volcanic soils + slope stability.

Worked example: a B/.80,000 house ≈ B/.800 construction tax + B/.50 cartón + per-sheet + B/.8 timbres ≈ ~B/.908 in municipal charges (excludes professional fees). Does one custom home need an EIA? Usually no on an ordinary titled interior lot — but coastal/island/protected sites (Pedasí, Bocas) are materially more likely to trigger it, the swing factor between a 3-month and a 12-month permit phase.

Coast vs highland — the real difference is land tenure

  • Boquete / Volcán / David (Chiriquí): mostly titled, cleaner permitting; David needs a fresh Registro Público cert + Catastro cert, idóneo/JTIA seal, Bomberos + MINSA permits. Volcán is distrito Tierras Altas, a separate alcaldía from David; electricity is EDEMET-EDECHI.
  • Pedasí (Los Santos / Azuero): permits via Municipio de Pedasí (fees not confirmed; extrapolated from Los Santos). The 22 m public-domain coastal strip applies.
  • Bocas: dominated by ROP and maritime concessions, not title. Over-water builds need land behind + a maritime concession + EIA. Hybrid ROP-land-plus-concession is common — careful legal review required.
  • Coastal law everywhere: the 22 m from high tide is public domain and cannot be titled (Ley 44/2006). ROP→title conversion needs proven possession >5 years + an MOP-registered survey, and cannot succeed in protected areas, comarcas, or concession zones.

Inspections, occupancy & property tax

  • Inspections + final inspection are by the municipal DOYC, not MIVIOT. Expat consensus: enforcement is light in practice — builder selection matters more than inspection (a Sora value-prop).
  • Permiso de Ocupación (occupancy): issued by the municipality, must be requested by the same party who pulled the construction permit (a reason to keep the builder bound to completion). Needs the Bomberos occupancy cert + updated Registro Público cert + builder Paz y Salvo. ~30–45 days.
  • Utilities (gated by the occupancy permit): electricity (occupancy copy + connection fee; ≥200A/3-phase/underground need a stamped plan) and water (Carta del Promotor for new property).
  • Register the improvement (mejoras) at the Registro Público for property tax; update values at ANATI first. IBI (Ley 66/2017, Vivienda Principal): first $120k exempt, 0.5% to $250k, 0.7% above. The old 20-year exoneration is legacy (permits before 2019 only).

Ready to break ground: ~3–7 months on a clean titled/highland lot; far longer where land tenure, environmental review, or coastal concessions are involved. Top delay causes, ranked: (1) land title / ANATI (ROP→title 1–3+ yr — the dominant Pedasí/Bocas risk); (2) plan-correction cycles (~3-month window or the file lapses); (3) environmental review; (4) multi-entity backlog; (5) rainy season — start in the dry season (Dec–Apr). A pre-designed model can cut 6–8 months off design/approval.
