How we get listings in Panama

Updated 2026-06-24 · Owner: Abraham · INTERNAL · companion to Competitive Intelligence
The "2,500+ listings" on a Panama brokerage homepage is mostly a display convention, not exclusive inventory. Casa Solution's own stats page admits ~300 signed listings in Boquete (and says that excludes MLS/co-broke) while the homepage says "2,500+." Panama's only real MLS (ACOBIR) covers under ~10% of the market, and brokering for commission legally requires a Panamanian corredor license. Volume is a vanity/low-trust metric here. Sora's leverage is the builder business + a small, genuinely-vetted curated portfolio — not a listing count.

1 · How the "2,500 listings" trick works

It's an aggregate, and the brokerages admit it. Casa Solution's homepage says "over 2,500 properties." Its Our Stats Explained page says "about 300 active signed listings in Boquete… does not include any MLS or cooperative agency listings." So exclusive signed inventory is roughly an order of magnitude below the displayed total.

The stack that makes a big number: own signed/exclusive listings (a few hundred at most) + ACOBIR co-broke pool + an IDX data feed pulled onto the brokerage's own site + syndication from classifieds (Encuentra24, Compreoalquile) + cross-region inventory + developer/pre-construction + stale FSBO. Heavy duplication results (same house, multiple agents, different prices). The tell: ACOBIR has only ~400 professionals and <10% coverage, so an ACOBIR feed alone can't make 2,500 — the rest is aggregation.

2 · Licensing reality (the real gate)

  • Credential: Licencia de Corredor de Bienes Raíces (Junta Técnica de Bienes Raíces / MICI). Law: Decreto Ley 6/1999 + DE 39/2001.
  • Natural person: Panamanian or foreigner with 5+ years permanent residence; B/.10,000 annual bond; clean record; pass the JTBR exam (Spanish-only, hard). 10+ yrs practice exempts the exam.
  • Foreign-owned SA: can hold a brokerage license, but its legal representative must personally be a licensed corredor. Foreign ownership is fine; the gate is the licensed rep.
  • Unlicensed but legal: owners selling their own property; developers' salaried in-house sales staff (paid salary/fee, not commission). Commission-tied work is reserved for the licensed. Referral fees to unlicensed persons = gray area — confirm with a Panamanian attorney.
  • Penalties: B/.10,000 fine for unlicensed brokering; 2024–25 MICI crackdown banned RE advertising without a visible license number; residency-loss risk cited for foreigners.
For Sora: cleanest structure = an SA whose licensed legal rep is a Panamanian (or 5-yr-resident) corredor who joins ACOBIR — OR run the builder side (no license needed) and partner with a licensed broker for listings. A non-resident foreigner can't personally be the licensed broker yet.

3 · The MLS landscape

  • MLS ACOBIR = the only real MLS (since 2012, ~400 pros), a cooperative co-broke pool that syndicates to "45+ portals" incl. Realtor.com. Coverage <10% of market; most agents don't use it.
  • No separate Chiriquí/Azuero MLS — ACOBIR only; regional coverage via local members. Bocas original licensed brokerage: Island Realty Panama (1994).
  • IDX feed available to licensed ACOBIR-member brokerages via approved vendors (e.g. iHomefinder), ~12h refresh — only after clearing the licensing gate.
  • Because there's no enforced MLS, most inventory is off-market (brokerages, Facebook groups, owners' heads). That's the problem (no comps) and the opportunity (direct sourcing works).

4 · Portals to be on

  • Tier 1 (in-country): Encuentra24 (dominant; RE is paid, ~$25/house-sale/90d; XML feed for brokerages), Compreoalquile (#2, Spanish-first).
  • Tier 2 (international syndication — the real lever for foreign buyers): Properstar / ListGlobally (50+ countries, 25 languages, feeds Rightmove/Realtor.com Intl/Idealista from one CRM feed), Point2Homes, Proppit/Lifull (icasas, Lamudi, Trovit, Mitula).
  • Tier 3 (niche, matters most): English-language brokerage sites with strong Google SEO beat Spanish classifieds for Boquete/Pedasí/Bocas because foreign buyers search in English. FSBO: YourPanama.com.
  • How to feed: one RE CRM (Wasi-type) as single source of truth → push to all portals. Agent/international pricing is sales-gated — unknown until quoted.

5 · Direct acquisition in the 4 micro-markets

  • FSBO is mainstream (no enforced MLS, seller-paid commission, small networked buyer pool). Yard-sign / drive-by discovery genuinely works.
  • FB / WhatsApp groups = the de facto MLS: Bocas Buy and Sell; Boquete/David/Chiriquí RE groups + Boquete.ning; Panama-wide "Panama Real Estate Buy Sell Rent." Pedasí-only group not web-discoverable — find on the ground; WhatsApp groups are referral-access only.
  • Lawyers & notaries as deal nodes: every transfer passes a notary + buyer's lawyer (title search, ROP-vs-titled check) — high-value off-market referral relationships.
  • Developers: Lucero / Boquete Canyon (Boquete), Costa Pedasi (Azuero), Nueva California (Volcán), Red Frog Beach (Bocas) — double as build-business partners.
  • THE critical issue — Rights of Possession (ROP) vs titled land: ROP = an occupancy right over state-owned land, not ownership, not in the Public Registry. ~85% of Bocas is ROP / ~15% titled. ROP = no bank financing, no title insurance, narrower buyer pool, harder resale, can be lost (pays no property tax). Also on Azuero/Pedasí coast + rural fincas. Foreigners can't own within 10 km of a border (near Volcán). Reputable practice (Sora's differentiator): written ROP-vs-titled confirmation + Registry cert + survey before any deposit, with an independent regional attorney.
  • Commissions: ~5% seller-paid by custom (3–6%, up to ~10% remote/island). Net listings are legal and common (agent keeps the spread above the owner's net; documented $100k+ markups) → listed prices unreliable; deal direct + independent appraisal. No central expiry → deep stale-FSBO inventory (esp. Pedasí buyer's market).

6 · The honest take + 90-day playbook

Don't chase listing volume. In Panama a high count is a low-trust commodity (no price transparency, legal net listings, the same house at 3 prices, MLS <10%). Aggregation is owned by Encuentra24 and incumbents, and "2,500" is mostly smoke. The leverage is the builder business + a small genuinely-vetted curated portfolio as the trust/lead engine. No one combines builder + licensed broker + curator + foreign-buyer lead-gen under one roof — that's Sora's whitespace. "Vetted" only works if real: verified title/ROP status, accurate pricing, disclosed credentials + independent legal due diligence.

Days 0–30 · Legal + positioning
  1. Stand up the SA + resolve licensing: a licensed Panamanian/5-yr-resident corredor as legal rep, or partner with a licensed broker for the listing side while Sora runs the builder side. Confirm referral-fee legality with an attorney.
  2. Decide on ACOBIR membership (~$75 + ~$55/mo) once a licensed broker is in place.
  3. Build the English-first curated-positioning site + one CRM (Wasi-type) as the single feed source.
  4. Define the Sora vetting standard (title/ROP verified, survey on file, price validated, attorney intro) — that is the product.
Days 30–60 · Direct sourcing
  1. Get into the FB groups; find the Pedasí / WhatsApp groups on the ground.
  2. Build referral relationships with 2–3 expat-closing attorneys + notaries per region.
  3. Work stale FSBO inventory for motivated, re-priceable sellers (esp. Pedasí).
  4. Open developer / pre-construction relationships (they double as build partners).
  5. Co-broke selectively with verified-licensed local agents for standout properties.
Days 60–90 · Curate, syndicate, convert
  1. Publish a small fully-vetted portfolio (~15–40, not thousands), each with title/ROP status disclosed and accurate pricing.
  2. Syndicate that feed: Encuentra24 + Compreoalquile + Properstar + Point2 + Proppit.
  3. Launch the content / lead-gen engine (YouTube + buyer guides) — it sources both buyers and sellers.
  4. Wire every listing inquiry into the build funnel: when no resale fits (common), offer a titled lot + custom build — the real margin.
