Materials & finishes by tier

Updated 2026-06-24 · Owner: Abraham · INTERNAL build playbook (2 of 4)
↖ Overview→ Cost & budget→ Process & timeline→ Architecture & permits→ Contractor & team
The core insight: structure is cheap and predictable (concrete, block, rebar are all local at government-tracked prices). Finishes are the budget wild card — mostly imported, up to ~5× North American prices, and where the three tiers actually separate. Tile alone swings from ~$1.20/sq ft to $60+/sq ft. And region drives the structure/envelope more than tier does: Pedasí + Bocas share a salt-corrosion regime; Boquete carries Panama's highest seismic hazard but needs almost no A/C.

Structure & envelope

  • Foundations: standard residential concrete f'c 3,000 PSI (4,000 available); ready-mix from CEMEX / Argos / Concretos del Pacífico, or bagged Argos Concrefácil (~52 sacks = 1 m³) for hard-access Bocas/remote Pedasí lots. Piles for soft/saturated soil and all over-water (palafito) Bocas builds. Coastal mix: low-permeability w/c ≤ 0.45.
  • Walls: concrete block (Panablock/Adoblock) for ESENCIAL; M2/EMMEDUE EPS panel (Ecotec Panamá) for SORA/FIRMA on hot coast/Bocas (~25% A/C savings, less relevant in cool Boquete); poured RC for high-spec/seismic.
  • Rebar: ASTM A615 Grade 60 for seismic structures (the default for Chiriquí/Boquete); supplier Feinsa.
  • Seismic code: REP-2021 (JTIA/MOP, in force since 30 Apr 2023). Boquete/Chiriquí is the highest-hazard zone in Panama (Ss ≈ 1.4–1.5); Pedasí and Bocas are lower-hazard.
  • Termite + humidity: subterranean termite can compromise structure in <5 years untreated. Standard package: pre/post-construction soil termiticide barrier, no wood-to-soil contact, sealed penetrations, moisture control, quarterly inspections.

Region envelope: Pedasí (salt air) — hot-dip galvanized steel, low-permeability concrete, 316-SS fasteners, marine-anodized aluminum. Bocas — piles + full corrosion regime + humidity/mold/termite pressure. Boquete/Volcán — seismic design dominates; manage mold; low A/C demand.

Windows & doors

  • No locally-made HVHZ "impact-rated" line exists — true impact certification is import-only (PGT/Andersen). Local market = tempered + laminated glass.
  • Coast (Pedasí/Bocas): anodized aluminum (25–100 micron), laminated security glass on sea-facing openings, casement/tilt-turn over sliders, 304-SS min / 316-SS beachfront hardware. Fabricators: Ventanas Vigil, Alumiglass, INVEFA (David: FUERTEC, Pinzón).
  • Highland (Boquete/Volcán): PVC + double-glazed (DVH) — INTRAPAK or Cochez (claims ~40% less solar heat gain) — the recommended highland spec for thermal/acoustic comfort.
  • Doors: Cochez and Do it Center both recommend metal (not wood) entrance doors in Panama's climate; anticorrosive metal/PVC near the sea.

Humidity rule (Pedasí/Bocas): engineered wood only over a slab with moisture barrier (slab RH <40%), never in wet areas. Better coastal alternative: vinyl plank (LVT/SPC). Suppliers: Cochez, Novey, Do it Center, ELMEC, Super Pisos (PTY); Do it Center + ELMEC in David.

Kitchen & bath

  • Countertops: ESENCIAL local granite / engineered quartz (MT, Cuarzo Stone) → SORA imported quartz (Caesarstone / Silestone / Compac) → FIRMA Italian/natural marble + Neolith (via Grupo Graymar). Bought/fabricated in PTY; budget freight to David/Boquete.
  • Fixtures (Novey/Cochez/Rodelag): FV (value) → Grohe / Hansgrohe → Dornbracht (special-order).
  • Sanitaryware: American Standard → Kohler (one-piece Reach toilet ~$412, a good SORA benchmark) → Toto / Duravit.
  • Appliances: RCA/Hisense/Frigidaire (RCA 6-burner stove ~$250) → Samsung/LG/Bosch (Multimax/Rodelag) → Miele (Miele Center Panamá) / Sub-Zero / Wolf.
  • Cabinets: El Ebanista 507 (moisture-resistant melamina for coast/Bocas), Tandor → laminate → lacquered / European (Carpenter México). All custom-quote, free 3D.

Systems

  • Electrical: 120/240V split-phase 60Hz, US plugs (US appliances work directly). ≤25 kVA → up to 125A; ≥200A or 3-phase/underground triggers a utility-approved electrical plan. Utility by region: Naturgy/EDEMET = Pedasí/Los Santos; Naturgy/EDECHI = Chiriquí/Boquete + Bocas; ENSA = east. Rural voltage instability drives generator + solar adoption (ATS mandatory).
  • Water: reserve tank + pump + pressure tank near-universal (IDAAN unreliable); UV-C for potability. Bocas = rainwater catchment.
  • Wastewater: septic is the norm outside Panama City (no sewer in Pedasí/Boquete/Bocas). Panama vendors use a 2-compartment tank; ≥2m from structures.
  • A/C: inverter mini-splits dominate, spec SEER 16+ (Novey: 12k ~$300–340, 24k ~$660–950). Pedasí/Bocas need substantial A/C; Boquete/Volcán need little or none — passive cooling is a selling feature.
  • Solar/off-grid: net metering since 2016; Law 417 exempts solar PV from import duty + VAT. Turnkey 5–7kW ~$9.5–13.5k, 10–12kW ~$16.8–22.5k. LiFePO4 batteries (~10-yr life) ~$650–700/usable kWh. Bocas off-grid is standard.
  • Starlink: legal nationwide, ideal for rural Pedasí + Bocas; ~$200–270 hardware, ~$35–55/mo.

Sourcing logistics

  • Big-box trio: Cochez, Novey, Do it Center (+ Hopsa distributor). David is well-served by all three and is the supply hub for Boquete/Volcán (~1 hr).
  • Pedasí/Azuero: source from Chitré (largest peninsula selection) / Las Tablas; PTY (~4.5 hr) for specialty.
  • Bocas: ~25–30% cost premium (mountain trucking + boat/barge; only Isla Colón has roads). Many builders truck from David.
  • Duties: 7% ITBMS (VAT); materials duty ~5–10%; US–Panama TPA: ~87% of US-origin goods enter duty-free (big lever for US buyers). Wooden articles need an ISPM15 permit before arrival (lead-time trap).
  • Local vs import: local = cement, block, rebar, basic aluminum, national tile, tanks, mini-splits. Import = high-end tile, stone slabs, European glazing, synthetic thatch, Miele/Sub-Zero, luxury fixtures (plan months, up to ~5× US prices).

What to highlight to a foreign buyer

ESENCIAL — "Solid, smart, turnkey"

  1. Engineered concrete-block structure to REP-2021 seismic code (not a wood box).
  2. Galvalume roof rated 20–50 yr with coastal-spec fasteners.
  3. Turnkey + national porcelain throughout, move-in ready.
  4. Water security baked in (reserve tank + pump + UV, independent of IDAAN).
  5. Inverter mini-split A/C sized per room; termite soil barrier from day one.

SORA (hero) — "Cooler, quieter, better finishes"

  1. M2/EMMEDUE insulated envelope — ~25% lower A/C bills on the coast; faster, stronger, quieter.
  2. Large-format imported porcelain + imported quartz counters + Kohler/Grohe.
  3. Laminated security glazing + 316-SS marine hardware (coast); PVC double-glazed in Boquete.
  4. Samsung/LG/Bosch package + Cat6/fiber wiring (FTTH where available, Starlink-ready elsewhere).
  5. Indoor-outdoor living + a documented, named builder (a real differentiator in a lightly-regulated market).

FIRMA — "Bespoke, off-grid, hospitality-grade"

  1. Imported marble / 120×120 gran-formato + lacquered/European cabinetry.
  2. Marine-anodized impact or European tilt-turn glazing.
  3. Signature synthetic-thatch palapa (50-yr life) or standing-seam/clay roof.
  4. Full off-grid resilience — solar + LiFePO4 + generator + well/rain catchment (duty/VAT-exempt under Law 417).
  5. Miele/Sub-Zero/Wolf + Toto/Dornbracht; infinity pool + architect-led indoor-outdoor design.
Pricing caveat: almost no Panama supplier publishes per-m²/per-unit prices for windows, stone, fixtures, or roofing — those are quote-only. The firm public anchors are tile (ELMEC/Do it Center), block walls (Contraloría 2024), rebar/polished concrete/PVC windows (CYPE, modeled), and A/C/sanitaryware (Novey/Kohler).
