*Internal reference. Not linked from any public nav. Treat volume claims as directional, not exact. Recommend connecting a real keyword tool to sharpen this before heavy content investment.*

## The filter that matters more than "is it popular"

Every LATAM retirement/expat hotspot already has real estate blog content. The question isn't "does demand exist" (it does, everywhere below), it's **where does SORA's specific angle — fixed-price turnkey new construction, not brokerage of existing homes — actually win a ranking fight.** Three filters, applied to every candidate:

1. **Is the "build" long-tail underserved relative to the "buy" long-tail?** Every market has resale-listing sites (Zillow-style MLS clones). Far fewer have real, current, credible *construction cost and process* content. That gap is where a builder (not a broker) has a natural authority advantage.
2. **Is titled land the norm, not the exception?** Steer toward zones where fee-simple title is standard (kills a large share of coastal Panama/CR, most of Belize's cayes, ejido-adjacent Mexico). SORA's own trust pitch (real title, real permits, real team) is worthless in a zone dominated by rights-of-possession or concession land — don't build authority content for markets that undermine the pitch.
3. **Is there a "value/build-your-own" story or only a "buy luxury resale" story?** SORA's Panama content already wins on this (Boquete/Volcán/Pedasí over Panama City). The same instinct should drive every new-market pick: the *emerging, cheaper, land-available* town next to the famous, saturated one — not the famous one itself.

## Cross-market long-tail patterns that consistently work

Independent of country, these keyword *shapes* showed real, low-competition opportunity in every market researched — use this as the template for every new guide's H2/H3 structure and title tag:

1. **"cost to build a house in [town] [year]"** — the single strongest informational long-tail across every market; almost nobody publishes real, current per-m²/per-sqft numbers with sourcing, most content is vague or outdated.
2. **"[emerging town] vs [famous town]"** — e.g. "Atenas vs Puriscal," "Boquete vs Volcán" (already have this as boquete-vs-pedasi, could add a Volcán version), "Ambergris Caye vs Corozal." Comparison posts rank for both head terms at once and match how buyers actually search once they've found the famous town and are looking for the value alternative.
3. **"buy land in [country] as a foreigner"** + **"titled land vs [rights of possession/ejido/ZMT] in [country]"** — the title-risk explainer is the highest-trust, most link-worthy content type, and it's exactly where a real builder/lawyer-backed team beats generic listing sites.
4. **"[visa name] + real estate"** — proven by SORA's own existing Friendly Nations Visa post. Every target country has an equivalent (Ecuador's $47K investor visa, Colombia's ~$150K M visa, Panama's own QIV). Each is a near-zero-competition long-tail pairing residency with the exact investment SORA sells.
5. **"[town] real estate 2026" market-update posts** — lower effort, high recency-SEO value, easy to refresh yearly, and matches the dated-content pattern (`-2026` in URLs/titles) that's already ranking well across nearly every competitor found in this research.
